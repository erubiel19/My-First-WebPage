Deja Vu

Deja vu

Déjà vu is the strange sense of having already experienced something, even if you know you haven't. Experts believe that this phenomena is most likely linked to memory in some manner. So, if you get déjà vu, it's possible that you've had a comparable experience previously. It's simply that you can't recall it.

Memory Loss

Déjà vu might be connected to differences in the brain's memory systems, causing sensory information to bypass short-term memory and instead reach long-term memory.

About the Brain

You could feel you're seeing something for the first time if your initial glimpse of it, such as a view from a hillside, didn't need your whole concentration. However, even if you weren't fully conscious of what you were seeing, your brain remembers the prior perception.

Past Life

It is thought that traumas from previous incarnations might manifest themselves in the present as dread or phobias. Some examples include dread of drowning, fear of a certain animal, fear of specific locations, and fear of a specific number, color, or item for no apparent reason.
